Barbie

Breaking Stereotypes, Inspiring Dreams

Barbie, the world's iconic fashion doll, has transcended generations, inspiring people to dream big, shatter stereotypes and embrace their true selves. Beyond her fashionable exterior, Barbie symbolises empowerment, resilience and limitless potential.

As Barbie's diverse careers unfold, she challenges traditional gender roles and reminds us that women can excel in any field, be it astronaut, doctor, president or athlete. She breaks barriers and empowers both girls and boys to reach for the stars.

Revolving around Barbie, her constantly evolving looks promote inclusivity with different skin tones, body types and hairstyles. She encourages acceptance and celebrates differences, sparking important conversations about diversity.

Beyond physical attributes, Barbie's adventures offer life lessons in perseverance and resilience. In addition, Barbie's commitment to education and creativity inspires young minds through science and art kits, reinforcing that knowledge is power and that girls can succeed in any field they choose.

Embodying more than just a doll, Barbie is an enduring symbol of female empowerment, shattering stereotypes and encouraging everyone to pursue their dreams. Embrace her feminist legacy as she reminds us all to embrace uniqueness, challenge norms and believe in ourselves, regardless of gender. Barbie's story of empowerment is one we should cherish and honour.

Some of the most important dates in Barbie's herstory:

  • 1959: Barbie's Debut - March 9, 1959: Barbie makes her debut at the American International Toy Fair in New York City. She was created by Ruth Handler, co-founder of Mattel, and named after her daughter, Barbara.
  • 1961: Ken Joins the Scene - Ken, Barbie's iconic boyfriend, is introduced to the Barbie lineup.
  • 1963: Midge Arrives - Barbie's best friend, Midge, is introduced as another character in the Barbie universe.
  • 1965: Barbie Goes to Space - Barbie takes her first step into the realm of career dolls with the release of "Miss Astronaut Barbie," inspired by the space race of the 1960s.
  • 1967: Twist 'n Turn Barbie - Twist 'n Turn Barbie is introduced, featuring a more flexible body that can pose more realistically.
  • 1971: Malibu Barbie - Malibu Barbie becomes a sensation, representing a sun-kissed, beach-loving lifestyle.
  • 1980: Barbie's First African American Doll - The first African American Barbie, known as "Black Barbie," is introduced, marking an important milestone in diversifying the Barbie lineup.
  • 1983: Barbie's Dream House - The iconic Barbie Dream House is introduced, featuring a pink three-story mansion with a working elevator.
  • 1992: Barbie as a Presidential Candidate - "Barbie for President" is released, featuring Barbie as a presidential candidate, inspiring young girls to dream big.
  • 1997: "I Can Be" Career Series - The "I Can Be" series is launched, emphasizing Barbie's career-oriented image with various professions.
  • 2001: Barbie's Film Debut- Barbie made her film debut in 2001, starring in Barbie in the Nutcracker, making her more popular than ever.
  • 2009: Barbie's 50th Anniversary - Barbie celebrates her 50th anniversary with special editions, commemorating her enduring legacy.
  • 2016: Barbie's Evolution - Barbie undergoes a significant transformation, introducing more diverse body types, skin tones, and hairstyles to better reflect the modern world.
  • 2019: Barbie's 60th Anniversary - Barbie reaches her 60th anniversary, with celebrations and special releases honoring her cultural impact.
  • Ongoing: Career Expansions - Barbie continues to expand her career options, from robotics engineer to firefighter, encouraging girls to explore various fields.

"My whole philosophy of Barbie was that, through the doll, the little girl could be anything she wanted to be. Barbie always represented the fact that a woman has choices."
-- Barbie creator Ruth Handler
